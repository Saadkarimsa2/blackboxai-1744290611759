Causes & Effects of Inflation

Exploring the various factors that drive inflation and their impact on the economy and society.

Major Causes of Inflation

Monetary Factors

  • Increase in money supply by central banks
  • Low interest rates leading to increased borrowing
  • Currency devaluation affecting import prices

Demand-Side Factors

  • Increased consumer spending
  • Rising government expenditure
  • Growing export demand

Supply-Side Factors

  • Rising production costs
  • Supply chain disruptions
  • Natural disasters affecting production

Structural Factors

  • Changes in tax policies
  • Government regulations
  • Market structure changes

Effects of Inflation

Economic Effects

Purchasing Power

Reduction in the buying power of money, affecting savings and fixed-income earners.

Economic Growth

Can lead to uncertainty, affecting investment decisions and economic planning.

International Trade

Impacts export competitiveness and balance of payments.

Social Effects

Income Distribution

Inflation can widen the wealth gap, affecting different social groups disproportionately. Fixed-income groups and savers typically suffer more than those with variable incomes or real assets.

Standard of Living

Rising prices can lead to reduced living standards, particularly affecting lower-income households. This may result in changes in consumption patterns and lifestyle adjustments.
