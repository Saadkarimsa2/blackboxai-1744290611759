What is Inflation?

Understanding the fundamental concept that shapes our economy and affects our daily lives.

Definition & Basic Concept

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, consequently causing purchasing power to fall over time. It's a sustained increase in the aggregate or general price level in an economy.

When inflation occurs, each unit of currency buys fewer goods and services than it did in prior periods. In other words, it reduces the value of money over time.

Types of Inflation

Demand-Pull Inflation

Occurs when aggregate demand exceeds available supply. This happens when consumers and businesses want to purchase more goods than the economy can produce.

Cost-Push Inflation

Results from increases in production costs, such as raw materials or wages, which are passed on to consumers through higher prices.

Built-In Inflation

Related to expectations of future inflation, where workers demand higher wages to keep up with rising costs of living, creating a wage-price spiral.

Measuring Inflation

Common Measures

  • Consumer Price Index (CPI)

    Measures changes in the price level of a weighted average market basket of consumer goods and services.

  • Producer Price Index (PPI)

    Measures average changes in selling prices received by domestic producers for their output.

  • GDP Deflator

    A measure of the level of prices of all new, domestically produced, final goods and services in an economy.
